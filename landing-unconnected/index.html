Problem

Is there a better way to find a great Data Science job.

 
  • Job titles are not consistent
  • Keywords like "data" can mean too general
  • Resumes highlight the skills used in previous jobs, not the ones you want to use in a new job.

Our Solution in 3 Easy Steps:

 

(1) Start with job descriptions you like.

(2) Emphasize the job characteristics you want.

(3) Find jobs that match this new job description.

Data Collection

 
  • More than 1,000,000 job ads have been accumulated - that's over 3 Gbs
  • Jobs pulled daily and stored in MongoDB
  • Limited to 10000 results, so pulled in batches by zipcode
  • No filtering - all jobs from truck driver to CEO is pulled
    • Less than 20% of all jobs collected contained the word "data"

Architecture

Text Pre-Processing

 

Filter Noise

  • Used inclusion and exclusion patterns:
    • Inclusion: Data Science Central research found 400 job titles for data scientists
    • Exclusion: Classification of jobs that clearly do not fall into data science category.
 

Text Normalization

  • Text normaliztion techniques before building features:
    • Remove non-alphanumeric characters, URLs and email addresses
    • Text converted to lower case
    • Remove stop words
    • Stemming (Snowball stemmer) to reduce words to root
  • Size of relavent jobs reduced to 250M from 400M.
  • Current model does not use stemming; some keywords were lost in the process.
